Projekt Velox

Project Velox is a project focused on the development of equipment in the field of particle physics, specifically on the development of a linear particle accelerator that will accelerate alpha particles to approximately 6% of the speed of light. The project is still in progress; we are currently in the first phase, which involves purchasing components and building the "head" of the accelerator. The project began in May 2024, and the project founders are students from Wichterlovo gymnázium in Ostrava, specifically Richard Komínek, David Zapletal, Hynek Uvíra, and Ondřej Martinák. The project is being realized in cooperation with VSB – Technical University of Ostrava, Proxima z. s., the company Edwards Vacuum, and Hyundai Motor Czech s.r.o. After the start of the project, we wrote a document with information about the project, which we sent through a teacher at our school, to Prof. Petr Alexa PhD who later became our mentor. however, we later, discovered that the document contained many errors and was outdated, so we will no longer be using it. After meeting with Prof. Alexa, we were given the opportunity to visit the neutron generator at VŠB-TUO and also obtained our own room for work. Although at that time it was not a laboratory but rather a storage room, we later received a laboratory in the FEI building. Even though we are working on a physics project, we are not all just physicists; we also have among us a programmer (David Zapletal), who is responsible for IT and communication with devices, a dedicated engineer (Richard Komínek), Ondřej Martinák, who takes care of documentation and helps with calculations, and of course Hynek Uvíra, who is a theoretical physicist and is the only one in the team capable of understanding the physical phenomena we are working with.
